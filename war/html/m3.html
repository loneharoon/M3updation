M3 Nomenclature

The M3 Nomenclature describes sensor metadata in a interoperable way (sensor type, measurement type, unit and domains).
By following the M3 nomenclature, it is easier to interpret sensor data and build interoperable IoT applications.

M3 Converter

We convert IoT data according to the M3 nomenclature. See M3 converter (convert SenML into M3 RDF data).

M3 Ontology & interoperable domain knowledge

The M3 nomenclature has been implemented in our M3 ontology.
The M3 Ontology references more than 30 sensors, measurements, units and about 10 domains.
The M3 ontology is focused on the ssn:ObservationValue concept from the W3C SSN ontology which describes sensors and observations, and related concepts. As explained by W3C SSN (final report):
  • The W3C SSN ontology does not describe domain concepts, time, locations, etc. these are intended to be included from other ontologies via OWL imports.
  • Future work: Standardise the SSN ontology to bridge the Internet of Things
  • Future work: Standardise the SSN ontology to use it in a Linked Sensor Data context
In the M3 ontology, we are focused on linking domain ontologies and rules to reason on sensor data.

M3 hub to combine cross-domain ontologies

The Machine-to-Machine Measurement (M3) hub interlinked cross-domain knowledge (ontologies, datasets and rules) as follows:
  • Link ontologies in the same domain
  • Link cross-domain ontologies
  • Link rules thanks to the Sensor-based Linked Open Rules (S-LOR) to interpret IoT data.
